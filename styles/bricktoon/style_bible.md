# Bricktoon Style Bible

## Core Look

- Blocky toy cartoon characters in a miniature plastic world.
- Rounded edges, expressive simple faces, readable props, cinematic lighting.
- 9:16-safe composition for shorts and vertical documentary cuts.
- Playful visually, serious in facts.

## Legal And Brand Safety

- No official LEGO branding or logos.
- No copyrighted characters or packaging.
- No real company logos unless intentionally sourced and legally supported.
- No real victim faces in dramatizations.
- Use fictional composite characters for victims, brokers, operators, and regulators.

## Character Design

- Short blocky bodies with rounded plastic edges.
- Dot eyes or simple cartoon eyes.
- Clear costumes that communicate role quickly.
- Avoid exact minifigure proportions.
- Use generic folders, badges, clipboards, trucks, and document props.

## Business Crime Visual Language

- Documents, invoices, receipts, phone screens, boxes, trucks, maps, and storage units.
- Plain white or generic rental-style vehicles with no private data.
- Official proof belongs in source cards, not fake legal documents.

## Camera Style

- Slow push-ins for tension.
- Quick zooms for price jumps.
- Overhead desk shots for paperwork.
- Wide shots for loading scenes.
- Close-ups for phones, invoices, and checklist reveals.

## Caption Style

- Big, bold, centered captions.
- 3-7 words per chunk.
- Avoid covering faces.
- Emphasize danger words sparingly: hostage, deposit, cash, too late, fake reviews.
