# Manual Notes

## Fixture Purpose

- This is a regression-test research note pack for the `test_story_template` topic.
- It is intentionally generic and fictional so pipeline changes can be tested without relying on a live publish story.
- The goal is to exercise research parsing, script generation, bricktoon cast logic, scene cards, visuals, render planning, and QC blockers.

## Story Frame

- Generic service business with a low quote or low upfront price.
- The customer thinks they found a deal.
- Pressure appears after commitment, when reversing course gets harder.
- Official proof should come from consumer-protection or regulator-style source cards.
- Dramatization should stay fictional and reusable.

## Candidate Research Angles

- How a normal-looking service quote can hide later fees or leverage shifts.
- Why urgency makes customers vulnerable after scheduling, loading, signing, or deposit payment.
- How to explain the difference between a legitimate estimate and a vague price teaser.
- How official agencies describe complaint patterns, warning signs, or enforcement language.
- What red flags viewers can check before booking a service.

## Candidate Source Types

- Consumer-protection agencies
- State attorney general guidance
- FTC-style warning pages
- Industry regulator pages
- Public complaint databases
- Court or enforcement summaries if later needed

## Candidate Facts To Validate

- Some service complaints follow a pattern where the quoted price changes after commitment.
- Deposits, vague paperwork, or verbal-only quotes can reduce customer leverage.
- Official guidance often recommends written estimates, business verification, and reviewing complaint history.
- A useful red-flag checklist can be built around quote clarity, payment demands, and proof of licensing or registration.

## Visual Opportunities

- Phone quote screen with suspiciously cheap offer
- Contract or invoice with revised totals
- Generic service truck or van with no branding
- Red-flag checklist card
- Official source card with exact verified wording

## Open Questions

- Which official-source pattern is the best stand-in for a generic service-trap story?
- Which fact language is broad enough for testing but still realistic?
- Which proof beat should become the standard QC checkpoint for this fixture?
